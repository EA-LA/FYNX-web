How to use sentiment correctly

Sentiment should support your setup, not replace it. A bullish reading does not mean buy everything. It means the environment may be more favorable for long ideas if structure also agrees.

When sentiment becomes dangerous

Extreme optimism or extreme fear can create traps. When everybody is leaning the same way, price can move sharply against the crowd to clear liquidity.

What Sentiment Measures

Trader emotion Shows how confident, fearful, aggressive, or defensive the market crowd is acting.
Crowd positioning Helps reveal whether traders are stacked heavily on one side of the market.
Momentum pressure Can show whether current flows support continuation or suggest exhaustion.

Best Use Cases

Bias confirmation Use it to confirm whether your long or short idea has market participation behind it.
Trade filtering Helps avoid weak setups when the crowd mood clearly fights your direction.
Trap awareness Extreme readings can warn of squeeze risk, reversal risk, or exhaustion risk.

Reminder

Never trade sentiment alone Always combine sentiment with structure, risk management, timing, and confirmation.
