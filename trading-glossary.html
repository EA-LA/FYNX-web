A B C D E F G H I L M O P R S T V

A

Core terms starting with A.

Ask Price

Forex

The price at which a seller is willing to sell an asset. In trading platforms, this is usually the price you buy at.

ATR

Technical

Average True Range. A volatility indicator that shows how much price typically moves over a chosen period.

B

Price movement, order flow, and directional terms.

Bearish

Market Bias

A view that price is likely to move lower. A bearish trader expects weakness or downside continuation.

Bid Price

Forex

The price a buyer is willing to pay for an asset. In many platforms, this is the price you sell at.

Breakout

Technical

When price moves beyond a key support, resistance, range, or pattern boundary with momentum.

Bullish

Market Bias

A view that price is likely to move higher. A bullish trader expects strength or upside continuation.

C

Structure, charting, and market flow terms.

Candlestick

Charting

A price bar showing open, high, low, and close for a time period. Candles help traders read momentum and structure.

Confluence

Strategy

When multiple reasons support the same trade idea, such as structure, trend, session, and indicator agreement.

Correction

Price Action

A temporary move against the main trend before possible continuation in the original direction.

Correlation

Risk

A relationship between two assets that tend to move together or against each other. Correlation matters for exposure control.

D

Risk and performance terms starting with D.

Drawdown

Risk

The decline from an account peak to a lower point. It measures how much the account has fallen during a losing period.

Divergence

Technical

When price and an indicator move differently, sometimes hinting at weakening momentum.

E

Execution and market event terms.

Entry

Execution

The exact point where a trader opens a position in the market.

Earnings

Stocks

A company’s financial report period. Earnings releases can create major volatility in stock prices.

Exposure

Risk

The total amount of market risk currently open in one or multiple positions.

F

Fast-moving concepts used daily by traders.

FOMO

Psychology

Fear of Missing Out. Emotional pressure to chase a move because it already started without you.

Fundamental Analysis

Macro

Analyzing markets using economic data, company performance, rates, inflation, and broader financial conditions.

Futures

Markets

Contracts that allow traders to speculate on or hedge the future price of an asset such as indices, oil, or gold.

G

Gap and growth related terms.

Gap

Stocks

When price opens significantly above or below the previous close, often after news or earnings.

H

Structure and protection terms.

Hedging

Risk

Taking another position to reduce or offset existing market risk.

Higher High

Structure

A price high that is above the previous high, often used to confirm upward trend structure.

I

Invalidation and indicator language.

Indicator

Technical

A mathematical tool placed on a chart to help analyze momentum, trend, volatility, or conditions.

Invalidation

Risk

The price point or condition that proves a trade idea is wrong.

L

Liquidity, leverage, and downside terms.

Leverage

Risk

Borrowed buying power that allows a trader to control a larger position with less capital. It increases both opportunity and danger.

Liquidity

Market Flow

How easily an asset can be bought or sold without causing large price movement.

Lot Size

Forex

The unit size used in forex trading to define position volume.

Lower Low

Structure

A price low that is below the previous low, often used to confirm downward trend structure.

M

Broad terms used across all markets.

Margin

Risk

The amount of capital required to open and maintain a leveraged trade.

Market Cap

Stocks/Crypto

Total market value of a company or cryptocurrency, usually calculated by price multiplied by supply or shares outstanding.

Moving Average

Technical

An indicator that smooths price over time to help identify trend direction.

O

Options and order terms.

Options

Markets

Contracts that give the right, but not the obligation, to buy or sell an asset at a set price before expiration.

Order Block

Price Action

A price area some traders view as a zone of institutional activity or strong historical reaction.

P

Execution and measurement terms.

Pip

Forex

A standard unit of price movement in forex pairs.

Position Size

Risk

The amount of an asset you trade, based on account size, stop loss distance, and accepted risk.

Pullback

Price Action

A temporary move against the current short-term trend before potential continuation.

Profit Target

Execution

A planned price level where a trader expects to take profit.

R

Risk and reward terms every trader should know.

Resistance

Technical

A price area where selling pressure has historically been strong enough to slow or reject price.

Retracement

Price Action

A temporary move against the main trend that does not necessarily change the overall direction.

Revenge Trading

Psychology

Emotional trading after a loss, usually driven by the desire to win money back immediately.

Risk-to-Reward Ratio

Risk

The comparison between how much you are risking and how much you aim to make on a trade.

S

Structure, spreads, and protection terms.

Scalping

Style

A fast trading style focused on small price moves and short holding times.

Slippage

Execution

The difference between the expected trade price and the actual filled price.

Spread

Forex

The difference between the bid price and ask price.

Stop Loss

Risk

A protective order or predefined level where the trade is closed to limit loss.

Support

Technical

A price area where buying pressure has historically been strong enough to slow or reject downside movement.

Swing Trading

Style

A trading style focused on multi-day to multi-week price moves.

T

Trend, technical, and timing terms.

Take Profit

Execution

A target order used to close a trade in profit at a planned level.

Technical Analysis

Technical

Analyzing charts, patterns, structure, and indicators to make trading decisions.

Trend

Structure

The overall direction of price movement over time, such as bullish, bearish, or ranging.

Trendline

Charting

A line drawn on a chart to connect key highs or lows and visualize direction or structure.

V

Volatility and volume terms.

Volatility

Market Condition

The speed and size of price movement. Higher volatility means price is moving more aggressively.

Volume

Technical

The amount of trading activity over a period. It can help confirm strength or weakness behind price movement.

Know the language. Read the market better.

A trader who understands the language of the market makes better decisions, reads setups faster, and avoids confusion during execution.
