Heatmap guide

Green zone

Supportive macro conditions. Usually signals healthier demand, resilient data, or a stronger policy backdrop.

Amber zone

Mixed readings. No clean edge yet. Traders often need one more catalyst before taking a stronger bias.

Red zone

Weak, fragile, or disappointing data. Often bearish for growth-sensitive assets and vulnerable currencies.

How to use it

1. Start with the largest contrast

The biggest macro gap between two regions often creates the cleanest forex relative-value setup.

2. Match macro with central-bank tone

Strong data plus hawkish guidance can reinforce currency strength. Weak data plus easing can intensify downside.

3. Wait for the next catalyst

Use CPI, jobs, PMI, retail sales, or GDP to confirm whether the heatmap trend is continuing or breaking.
