Heatmap guide

Green zone

Healthy or supportive prints. Can imply relative macro strength, policy flexibility, or stable demand.

Amber zone

Mixed data. Usually means no clean macro edge yet and traders should wait for confirmation.

Red zone

Weak prints, contraction risk, or broad disappointment. Often bearish for growth-sensitive assets.

How to read it

Step 1: Find strongest and weakest regions

Compare rows quickly. The biggest contrast often creates the cleanest FX relative-value setup.

Step 2: Match with central bank bias

Strong data plus hawkish tone can strengthen a currency. Weak data plus easing can pressure it.

Step 3: Wait for catalyst

Use the next CPI, jobs, PMI, or GDP release to confirm whether the macro direction is continuing.

Best trading uses

Forex

Compare two economies directly before trading EUR/USD, GBP/USD, USD/JPY, or CHF pairs.

Indices

Weak growth plus soft earnings outlook can weigh on stock indices and risk appetite.

Bonds

Cooling inflation and weaker activity may support bond prices if cuts become more likely.
